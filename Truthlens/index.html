How It Works

Articles are cleaned, stopwords removed, and lemmatised. TF-IDF converts text to 50,000-dimensional feature vectors. A Support Vector Machine classifies and returns a probability score.

📊

The Model

Trained on the ISOT dataset — 44,032 real and fake news articles. Achieved 99.48% F1-score, outperforming BiLSTM and RoBERTa on this benchmark. Cross-tested on WELFake: 87.17%.

🔍

Explainability

LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) identifies which specific words pushed the model towards its verdict, making every decision transparent and auditable.

⚠️

Limitations

Trained primarily on political and world news in English. Performance may vary on social media posts, opinion pieces, or content written very differently from traditional news journalism.
